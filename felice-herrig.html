    Felice Herrig – Early Career in Kickboxing

    Before she became the “Lil’ Bulldog” that we know, Felice pursued her early career as a professional kickboxer holding a record of 23-5.

    Currently, Herrig is ranked number 2 in the World in the IKF (International Kickboxing Federation) Pro Women’s Muay Thai Bantamweight Division. Not to mention, she even has an IKF title to vouch for it.

    Similarly, she got the title after winning against Katie Meehan by unanimous decision on November 15, 2008. Other than that, she is also a two-time International Kickboxing Federation open Tournament Champion. 

    Felice’s first amateur win was against Terri French of Little Rock, Arkansas, back in 2005. There she became the 2005 IKF North American Classic Amateur Full Contact Rules Bantamweight Tournament Champion.

    The following year, Herrig defended the title against Stacy Chung of Thunder Bay, Ontario, Canada.

    Felice Herrig – Entry to MMA aka Mixed Martial Arts

    After her successful kickboxing career, Felice then competed on Fight Girls in 2007 and won her fight against a Thai fighter.

    Likewise, Herrig officially made her MMA debut on February 21, 2009, at UWC: Man “O” War, where she lost to Iman Achhal. Following the defeat, her next fight was with Michele Gutierrez at Unconquered 1: November Reign, which she won.

    Moreover, Herrig then had an immensely successful MMA career as she kept winning the fights. In a short time, Herrig claimed a win against fighters like Jessica Rakoczy, Amanda LaVoy, Andrea Miller, and Nicadali Rivera-Catanoc, to name a few.

    Likewise, in December 2012, Felice was meant to face Michele Gutierrez in a rematch but instead competed with Patricia Vidonic after Michele withdrew. Nonetheless, Herrig won the match via unanimous decision.

    In her next fight, Felice faced Heather Clark at Bellator 94 in March 2013, which she won via split decision. However, a fight ensued post-match after Herrig unleashed a celebratory yell in her opponent’s face.

    Following her win in August 2013, Felice Herrig signed a multi-fight deal with Invicta Fighting Championships. There she faced Tecia Torres at Invicta FC 7: Honchak vs. Smith on December 7, 2013, which ended up in her loss.

    Not even a year later, Herrig was signed by the UFC along with ten other strawweight fighters to compete on season 20 of The Ultimate Fighter. So naturally, everyone there wanted to be the first-ever UFC women’s strawweight champion.

    Likewise, Herrig was coached by Anthony Pettis but later lost the match in the quarterfinal round of the competition.

    Felice Herrig – Professional UFC Career
