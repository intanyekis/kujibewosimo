    A commanding figure in the realm of entertainment with an estimated net worth of $120 million, Joseph James Rogan, better known as Joe Rogan, has fashioned a very successful career as a comedian, sports commentator, renowned podcaster, and the host of "The Joe Rogan Experience" podcast. He first became famous in the 90s as an actor on the TV show NewsRadio. But it was his podcast that really made him a star. It is a big hit worldwide and has added immensely to his wealth.

    Rogan usually talks about many things on his show but never really talks about his money. Despite this, he has occasionally let some aspects of his private lifestyle slip, revealing where some of his wealth is directed. These hints have given some idea of how he spends his money.

    9 Joe Rogan Invests A Lot On Recovery Treatments Such As Cryotherapy

    Joe Rogan takes his health very seriously and doesn't hesitate to spend big on recovery treatments. Cryotherapy is one treatment he often uses. He learned about this from Dr. Rhonda Patrick, a guest he brought on his podcast.

    Many of his friends from the UFC also use cryotherapy. For example, Eddie Bravo used it daily while preparing for a match. Another UFC fighter, Alan Jouban, used it twice daily to reduce knee inflammation before a fight. Inspired by these stories, Rogan has made cryotherapy a regular part of his routine.

    8 Joe Rogan Spends A Lot On Meat

    Joe Rogan is a big fan of protein and enjoys a diet filled with different types of meat. That's the reason why he bought a top-quality grill from Traeger Grill. This grill is so advanced it's like the Tesla of grills. According to The Richest, Rogan is also known for his love of bow hunting, especially for elk.

    He often talks about how elk meat is nutritious, organic, and tasty. This special type of meat costs $18.90 per pound, but Rogan enjoys it so much that he includes it in his meal daily.

    7 Joe Rogan Spends A Fortune On His Private Gym

    Fitness is a top priority for Joe Rogan. That's why he built a personal gym in his 14,000-square-foot warehouse, which is also where he records his podcast. The gym is packed with high-quality equipment that didn't come cheap.

    Rogan picked out the best items for his gym, mostly from high-end brands like Rogue and his own company, Onnit. This shows just how serious Rogan is about staying in shape, and he's willing to spend big on the best fitness gear on the market.

    6 Joes Rogan Makes Huge Investment In Wellness Products

    Joe Rogan doesn't hold back when it comes to his wellness and fitness. He's a leading figure for the Onnit brand and often promotes their products. One of their standout items is their uniquely shaped kettlebells.

    Apart from his workout gear, Rogan is also serious about his supplements. His diet includes a range of products, like Kombucha, a raw, organic green supplement. His investment in wellness and fitness is as significant as his dedication to it which is incredible.

    5 Joe Rogan Loves To Buy Expensive Watches

    Joe Rogan has a big love for watches, and he's put quite a bit of money into his collection. He owns a wide range of watches, from top luxury brands to more budget-friendly ones.

    According to Money Made, one of his priciest watches is a Rolex Yacht-Master 40 Ref. 16622, which cost him a hefty $70,000. But not all his watches are that expensive. He also has a Casio G-Shock Mudmaster GG-1000-1A3 that costs just $350.

    4 Joe Rogan Loves To Spend On Expensive Cars

    Joe Rogan has a great love for cars, which shows in his impressive car collection. He owns a mix of both classic and modern cars. According to The Richest, his collection includes a custom 1965 Corvette Stingray that has a strong V8 engine and costs about $125,000. He also owns a 2014 Ford Mustang and his very first car, a Toyota Supra MK IV.

    Other cars in his garage are a 1960 Ford Bronco, a BMW M3 worth $69,900, and a Porsche 911 GT3 RS that cost him $300,000. His latest addition is a Tesla Model S, which he claims is one of his best cars. This car cost him $100,000.

    3 Joe Rogan Invests In A Personal Sauna

    Joe Rogan believes in the power of a sauna for quick recovery. He doesn't use it once in a while; he uses it four times a week. Rogan has invested in a personal sauna at home as a part of his wellness routine.

    In one of his discussions, he mentioned his regular sauna usage, stating that he's been pushing the temperature to 200 degrees Fahrenheit and staying in for 20 minutes. The last 5 minutes are intense, but his body seems to adapt, making hot yoga seem less hot. This routine is part of his weekly regimen, which he claims makes him feel good.

    2 Joe Rogan Has An Expensive Firearms Collection

    According to The Richest, Joe Rogan has a noteworthy collection of firearms, which is a topic he often discusses on his podcast. Several of his firearms have been gifts from guests on his show.

    One of his pieces is the FN 509 Compact, which has a price tag of over $1,049. His collection, which is frequently showcased in the background of his segments, includes guns worth thousands of dollars. This collection is a testament to Rogan's interest in firearms.

    1 Joe Rogan Invests In Million-Dollar Homes

    Joe Rogan sure knows how to invest in comfort. According to The Richest, he dropped a whopping $14.4 million on a stunning mansion in Austin, Texas. This enormous house spread over 10,890 square feet, boasts eight bedrooms, ten bathrooms, and a beautiful pool. Its open-concept design seamlessly connects the living room, kitchen, and dining room.

    The rustic interior decor with Eastern furniture and floor-to-ceiling glass windows makes it a stylish retreat. The mansion also sports a professional gym, sauna, and steam room. But this isn't Rogan's first real estate investment.
