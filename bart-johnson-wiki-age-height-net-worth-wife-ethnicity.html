    Bart Johnson Age & Early Life

    Bart Johnson, born Barton Robert Johnson on December 13, 1970, in California, United States, is the son of Bob and Charlene Johnson, a television hairdresser. His parents own Johnson Mill Bed & Breakfast, and he has two brothers, Adam and Brad, who are also actors. Bart attended Wasatch High School and pursued pre-med at the University of Utah before earning a Bachelor of Arts from Yale University. He hails from an English-Scottish background.

    Career

    Growing up as the son of a hairdresser, Bart Johnson developed a fondness for shows like Mork and Mindy, The Brady Bunch, and Charlie’s Angels. His cinematic debut came with the role of Frosty in Crossroads in 1993, followed by a starring role in the TV series Saved by the Bell: The New Class the next year. He also appeared in the indie drama film My Family and as a guest on TV shows like Diagnosis Murder and Thunder Alley in 1995. Johnson’s career expanded to various TV shows including Jag, Sunset Beach, Babylon 5, and Walker, Texas Ranger.

    His breakthrough came with the role of Coach Jack Bolton in the High School Musical film series, featuring in all the television movie franchises. His TV credits include Clueless, CSI: Miami, Eve, Simon Says, Happy Valley, and NCIS, among others. Bart has also been part of notable films such as Evil Angel, The Cell 2, Beautiful Wave, The Saratov Approach, Little Women, and Desertion. Additionally, he has appeared in films like Simon Says, Taking Five, Daddy Day Camp, Las Vegas, Locker 13, and The Yankees. Bart purchased Johnson Mill Bed & Breakfast from his parents and later transformed it into a drug addiction treatment facility, receiving the AAA Four Diamond Award in 2012.

    Bart Johnson Wife & Marriage

    Who is Bart Johnson’s wife? Bart Johnson is married to actress Robyn Lively, and they tied the knot on September 25, 1999. The couple shares two sons, Baylen and Wyatt, and a daughter, Kate.
