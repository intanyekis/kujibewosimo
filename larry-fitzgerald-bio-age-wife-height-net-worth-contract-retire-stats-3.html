    Larry Fitzgerald Education

    Fitzgerald studied at the Holy Angels School in Richfield, Minnesota.where he also played highschool football. Whilst there, Fitzegerald became a two-time All-State First-Team receiver.

    Larry did not meet the NCAA criteria to play soccer as a student, so he spent a year at the Valley Forge Military Academy in Pennsylvania.Later on, Fitzgerald joined the University of Pittsburgh, where he played for the Pittsburgh Panthers football team under head coach Walt Harris.

    Larry Fitzgerald Cardinals

    On August 20, 2011, Fitzgerald agreed to sign an 8-year, $120 million contract with the Cardinals, linking him to Richard Seymour as the fifth most remunerated player in the NFL at the time. Fitzgerald’s first solid outing came against the Washington Redskins in Week 2, where he had seven games for 133 yards and a 22-21 loss touchdown.

    Two games later, he had eight catches for 102 yards in a 31–27 defeat to the New York Giants. On November 13, at Week 10, he had seven passes for 146 yards and four touchdowns in a 21–17 win over the Philadelphia Eagles. His efforts against the Eagles won him the honors of NFC Offensive Player of the Week.

    Fitzgerald’s achievements have been recognized by the All-Pro second team selection as well as his sixth Pro Bowl selection. He finished ranked seventh in the league among his peers on the 2012 NFL Top 100 Players list.
